Flight Delay Prediction

  • Category: Machine Learning
  • Client: Academic
  • Project date: Nov, 2022
  • Project URL: Car Prediction

Summary

  • The project aims to help predict flight delays to effectively manage air traffic. Two machine learning models were built and compared for prediction: K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) classification and Logistic Regression.

  • Background: Flight delays have become a prevalent issue, with the FAA managing over 40,000 daily flights in the US. Both the FAA and airlines are held responsible for delays, affecting operational efficiency, passenger satisfaction, and aviation safety. The project's objective is to build predictive models to aid the FAA in effectively managing flight delays and improving air traffic control.

  • Model1 (KNN): The model was evaluated based on Recall and Precision, considering operational implications like false positives and false negatives. The F-1 score at k=11 and threshold 0.40 was 0.40, indicating moderate performance. However, the model did not seem to perform well overall.

  • Model2 (Logistic Regression): The model was also evaluated using Recall and Precision, and the F-1 score at the probability threshold of 0.40 was 0.41, slightly better than KNN but still considered low.

  • Model Comparison ROC curves were used to compare the models based on the area under the curve (AUC). Logistic Regression outperformed KNN with an AUC of 0.57 compared to 0.53. F-1 score and ROC analysis suggested that Logistic Regression was the better model for this dataset.

  • Conclusion: Despite the best F-1 score of 51% at the probability threshold of 0.30, the models' overall performance indicated room for improvement. A better model should be sought to enhance prediction accuracy and reduce both false positives and false negatives, ultimately helping the FAA manage flight delays and air traffic more efficiently. .
